## Business Objective
Cyclistic aims to increase annual memberships by converting casual riders into long-term members.  
This analysis examines rider behavior differences to identify actionable conversion opportunities.

## Key Findings

- **Casual riders** take longer rides on average and primarily ride on **weekends**, indicating leisure usage.
- **Members** ride more frequently, especially on **weekdays**, suggesting commuting and routine-based behavior.
- Ride volume for both groups increases in warmer months, with a sharper seasonal spike among casual riders.
- Casual riders show peak usage during mid-day and afternoon hours, while members peak during commute times.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Target weekend casual riders** with limited-time membership trials.
2. **Seasonal promotions** during peak summer months to capture high casual usage.
3. Introduce **weekend or leisure-focused membership plans**.
4. Market annual memberships as a cost-effective option for frequent long-duration riders.

## Expected Impact
Converting even a small percentage of high-frequency casual riders could significantly increase recurring revenue and customer lifetime value.


## Linking Insights to Business Action

The analysis highlights clear behavioral differences between casual riders and annual members.  
To ensure insights translate into measurable impact, each recommendation is paired with a metric for evaluation.

|-------|----------------|----------------|
